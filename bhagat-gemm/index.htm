A revolutionary high strength Mg based alloys without rare earth addition for structural applications: Their structure property correlation.

Magnesium based alloys are known for its lightweight capabilities with limited strength. This research is aimed at enhancing the strength of Mg with suitable alloying additions. Generally, rare earth elements provide strength to the magnesium alloys. Since it is relatively high cost, alloying elements alternate to rare earth with low cost has been preferred. With strong literature review, a research proposal has been conceived.

Tribological properties of Magnesium based Bio material

Magneisum and its alloys are considered as the best replacement material for the human bones. However, problems such as high hydrogen evoultion and limited corrosion resistance limits its applications. To overcome these practical hurdles suitable alloying elements were identified. Moreover, the tribological properties of the magneisum based bio material to be tested under the controlled environment which is quite similar to the human body conditions.

An effort to study the densification and mechanical behaviour of magnesium powders sintered through microwave sintering.

It is general perception that magnesium-based alloys are highly reactive that cannot be sintered through conventional sintering techniques. To overcome this practical hurdle an exclusive microwave set up has been designed which is quite similar to advanced sintering furnaces. This is one of the first of its kind of furnaces which is not commercially available.
