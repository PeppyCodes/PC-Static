Career Objective

To be in an ambiance which thrives on the continuous and simultaneous development of research with strong Industry-Academic coaction. Career Summary

An eager beaver manufacturing engineer with an emphasis on material science, possess both practical and technical knowledge on some of the manufacturing and metal forming processes. Highly skilled in the liquid metallurgy process for the fabrication of aluminum or magnesium-based alloys or composites. Possess experience in the fabrication of Al based metal matrix composites through powder metallurgy route. Extensive knowledge of the metal forming process such as extrusion, rolling and severe plastic deformation is attested via publication records. Commanding knowledge is also stamped on the mechanical behaviour and various characterization techniques for materials such as Magnesium, Aluminium, Copper, Nickel and some ferrous alloys. Expert user of Tensile testing, Hardness test, Pin on disc machine and other mechanical testing equipment.

Tribological properties of Magnesium based Bio material

Magneisum and its alloys are considered as the best replacement material for the human bones. However, problems such as high hydrogen evoultion and limited corrosion resistance limits its applications. To overcome these practical hurdles suitable alloying elements were identified. Moreover, the tribological properties of the magneisum based bio material to be tested under the controlled environment which is quite similar to the human body conditions.

An effort to study the densification and mechanical behaviour of magnesium powders sintered through microwave sintering.

It is general perception that magnesium-based alloys are highly reactive that cannot be sintered through conventional sintering techniques. To overcome this practical hurdle an exclusive microwave set up has been designed which is quite similar to advanced sintering furnaces. This is one of the first of its kind of furnaces which is not commercially available.
